About Me

Background

Ph.D. student in Bioengineering at Clemson University under Dr. Renee Cottle. Transitioned from mechanical engineering (3.902/4.0 GPA) to cutting-edge genetic research, bringing interdisciplinary expertise to cellular engineering.

Research Focus

Specializing in ex-vivo gene editing and transplant research using CRISPR-Cas9 technology. Developing clinical-grade electroporation systems for treating inherited metabolic liver diseases through precise genetic modifications.

Technical Expertise

Proficient in cell culture, gene editing, electroporation, PCR, fluorescence imaging, 3D scaffolds, mechanical testing, and computational modeling using ANSYS, COMSOL, and various CAD software.
