About Me

Background

I am a dedicated Ph.D. student in Bioengineering at Clemson University, working under the guidance of Dr. Renee Cottle. My journey began with mechanical engineering, where I achieved a 3.902/4.0 GPA, before transitioning into the fascinating world of cellular and genetic engineering.

Expertise

I specialize in ex-vivo gene editing and transplant research, with proficiency in electroporation, cell isolation, PCR, fluorescence imaging, and advanced computational modeling. My interdisciplinary approach combines engineering principles with cutting-edge biotechnology.

Vision

My goal is to develop innovative gene therapy solutions for inherited metabolic liver diseases. Through CRISPR-Cas9 technology and clinical-grade electroporation systems, I aim to translate laboratory discoveries into life-changing treatments for patients.

Research

Current Focus: CRISPR-Cas9 Gene Therapy

My research centers on developing and validating clinical-grade electroporation systems for CRISPR-Cas9 mediated gene therapy in primary hepatocytes. This work aims to correct inherited metabolic liver diseases through precise genetic modifications, potentially offering new hope for patients with previously untreatable conditions.
